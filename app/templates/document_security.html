How Your Documents Are Secured

A plain-language walkthrough of how Credanta handles your files from the moment you upload them to the moment you share or delete them.

1

You upload a document

Files are uploaded directly to your account over an encrypted connection. Credanta accepts common document formats including PDF, Word documents, and images.

2

Documents are stored in private storage

Each user's files are stored in a private, isolated location. Documents belonging to one account are not accessible to other users. Files are not publicly hosted or indexed.

3

Metadata is used to organize your documents

Credanta stores metadata — such as document title, category, and expiration date — to help you stay organized. This metadata powers the dashboard, expiration alerts, and checklist views.

4

Access is limited to the account owner

Only you can view, download, or manage your documents. No other user, recruiter, or third party has access to your files unless you create a share link.

5

Share links are temporary and controlled by you

If you choose to share documents with a recruiter or employer, Credanta generates a private link. You control when and whether to share this link. The recipient can view the selected documents but cannot modify or delete them.

6

You can revoke access at any time

Share links can be revoked from your account. Once revoked, the link no longer grants access to any documents. Expired or revoked links return an access-denied response.

Important Trust Commitments

  • Your documents are not public by default. They require an explicit share action to be visible to anyone other than you.
  • Share links should only expose the selected documents for that share session.
  • Expired or revoked links no longer allow access to any files.
  • Sensitive files are never publicly indexed or discoverable by search engines.

What You Should Not Upload

Credanta is designed for professional career documents. Please do not upload:

  • Patient records or protected health information (PHI)
  • Documents you are not authorized to store or share
  • Personally identifiable information belonging to other people
  • Classified, confidential, or legally restricted records

Credanta is not a HIPAA-compliant platform and should not be used to store regulated health records.

Credanta is not an employer, recruiter, credentialing agency, or legal compliance service. Users are responsible for confirming document requirements with employers, agencies, schools, or licensing bodies.
