Credanta / Security

Security Overview

Credanta is designed to keep your professional documents private, organized, and accessible only to you. Here is how we approach security at each step.

Secure Sign-In

Authentication is handled via Google OAuth. Credanta does not store or manage passwords. Access to your account requires a valid Google session.

Private Document Storage

Uploaded documents are stored privately in per-user storage. Files are not accessible to other users and are not publicly indexed or exposed.

Controlled Share Links

Share links are created only when you choose. You can revoke access at any time. Expired or revoked links no longer grant access.

Expiration Reminders

Credanta tracks expiration dates for licenses and certifications and alerts you when credentials are approaching renewal — reducing compliance gaps.

Account Protection

Each account is fully isolated. Users can only access their own documents. Admin functions require verified credentials separate from regular accounts.

Responsible Data Handling

Credanta does not sell user data. Your documents are not used for advertising, profiling, or shared with third parties without your explicit action.

What We Do Not Claim

We are committed to honest communication. Credanta does not claim to be HIPAA-compliant, SOC 2 certified, or a legal compliance service. We are a professional document management tool, not a regulated health or legal platform.

Users are responsible for confirming document requirements with employers, licensing bodies, agencies, or schools.

Reporting a Security Concern

If you identify a potential security issue, please contact us at support@credantaapp.com. We take all reports seriously and will respond promptly.
