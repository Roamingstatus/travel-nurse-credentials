About Credanta

Credanta is a professional document management platform built to help modern professionals organize, monitor, and share important career documents.

What We Do

Professionals in healthcare, education, skilled trades, and other credentialed fields manage a constant stream of important documents — licenses, certifications, resumes, background checks, onboarding records, and more. These documents expire, get scattered across devices and emails, and need to be shared repeatedly with employers, agencies, and clients.

Credanta brings all of these into one organized, private, and shareable workspace. You track what you have, see what's expiring, and share exactly what's needed with a single link — no attachments, no chaos.

What You Can Store

Professional Licenses Certifications Resumes & CVs Background Check Records Onboarding Documents ID & Compliance Records Continuing Education Reference Letters

Built for Modern Professionals

Credanta was designed with travel nurses, healthcare professionals, educators, and contract workers in mind — anyone who manages a professional portfolio and needs to share documents quickly and reliably.

  • Expiration tracking — get notified before licenses lapse
  • Recruiter packets — share a clean document bundle with one link
  • Status at a glance — see what's current, expiring, and missing
  • Application-ready — no more hunting for the right file at the wrong time

Privacy First

Your documents are private by default. You choose what to share, when to share it, and with whom. Credanta does not sell your data, analyze your documents for advertising, or share your documents with third parties without your explicit action.

Who We Are

Credanta is built by NexusGarden, a product studio focused on practical tools for working professionals. We build software that respects your time, protects your data, and actually fits the way you work.

Credanta is not an employer, recruiter, document review service, or legal compliance service. Users are responsible for confirming document requirements with employers, agencies, schools, or licensing bodies.
